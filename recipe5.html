Chicken Biryani

Hard ⏱️ 90 minutes 👥 6 Servings

Description

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful one-pot rice dish from Indian cuisine. It features spiced chicken cooked with basmati rice, aromatic spices, and herbs. While it takes time, the result is absolutely worth it - a restaurant-quality dish that's perfect for special occasions or when you want to impress your guests.

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 800g Chicken, cut into medium pieces
  • 1 cup Plain yogurt
  • 3 tablespoon Ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 Green chilies, chopped
  • 2 tablespoon Lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoon Biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon Red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice:

  • 500g Basmati rice (long grain)
  • 6-7 Cups Water
  • 6-8 Whole cloves
  • 4 Black cardamom pods
  • 3 Green cardamom pods
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 1 Inch stick Cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon Black peppercorns
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoon Ghee or oil

For Layering and Cooking:

  • 3 Onions, sliced thin
  • 4 tablespoon Ghee or oil
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • 2 tablespoon Yogurt mixed with 1/2 teaspoon saffron soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken:

  1. Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, lemon juice, biryani masala, chili powder, turmeric, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Add chicken pieces and coat well. Marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 2 hours).

Step 2: Prepare the Rice:

  1. Wash basmati rice under running water until water runs clear. Soak for 20 minutes and drain.
  2. Boil 6-7 cups water in a large pot with whole cloves, black cardamom, green cardamom, bay leaves, cinnamon, black peppercorns, and salt.
  3. Add soaked rice to boiling water. Cook until 70% done - rice should have a slight bite (about 6-7 minutes). Drain well.

Step 3: Fry the Onions:

  1. Heat 3 tablespoons ghee in a pan. Fry sliced onions on medium-high heat until golden brown and crispy (about 8-10 minutes). Remove and keep aside.
  2. Reserve about 2 tablespoons of fried onions for garnish.

Step 4: Layer and Cook (Dum Pukht):

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot (or biryani handi), spread a thin layer of remaining ghee (about 1 tablespoon).
  2. Spread half of the marinated chicken on the bottom layer.
  3. Top with half of the cooked rice.
  4. Sprinkle half of the fried onions, half of fresh mint, and half of fresh coriander.
  5. Repeat layers: remaining chicken, rice, onions, mint, and coriander.
  6. Pour saffron soaked in yogurt over the top. Drizzle remaining ghee.
  7. Cover the pot with aluminum foil, then place the lid on top (to trap steam).
  8. Cook on high heat for 1-2 minutes until steam forms, then reduce to low heat.
  9. Cook for 45-50 minutes on low heat without opening the lid.
  10. Turn off heat and let rest for 5 minutes without opening the lid.

Step 5: Serve:

  1. Open lid carefully. Gently mix biryani with a fork - don't stir as it will break the rice.
  2. Transfer to serving dish and garnish with remaining fried onions, fresh mint, and coriander.

💡 Tips

  • Use good quality basmati rice for best results - longer grains work best.
  • Don't skip the marinating step - it ensures the chicken is flavorful and tender.
  • The key to perfect biryani is the "Dum Pukht" (steam cooking) - don't open the lid during cooking.
  • If you don't have a heavy-bottomed pot, use a pressure cooker for the final cooking step (cook on high pressure for 15 minutes after sealing).
  • Ghee gives better flavor than oil, so try to use it.
  • You can add potatoes, eggs, or shrimp to make variations.
  • Serve with raita (yogurt) and pickle for authentic taste.
